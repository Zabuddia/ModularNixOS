Project Purpose

I created this website to introduce people to the minimalist art movement and to share what I learned through my research. Each page explains major ideas, artists, and styles, and the backgrounds are digital designs I made based on the minimalist works I studied. The goal is to present the main concepts of minimalism in a simple, clear way while visually reflecting the movement's style.

Design Choices

The overall design of the website follows minimalist principles:

  • Clean, uncluttered layouts with plenty of open space.
  • A limited color palette inspired by minimalist painting.
  • Simple typography and straightforward page structure.
  • Background art based on works and styles by artists such as Stella, Kelly, Judd, and Truitt.
  • A small set of focused pages with no unnecessary visual elements.

These choices reflect key themes in minimalist art, including clarity, reduction, geometry, repetition, and an emphasis on color and space.

How to Use This Site

You can explore the pages in any order, but one simple path is:

  • Start at What Is Minimalism? for a basic introduction to the movement.
  • Visit Key Artists to learn about some of the most important minimalist figures.
  • Look at the Gallery to see examples of minimalist artworks by well-known artists.
  • Return to About (this page) to understand how the site was designed.
  • See the Bibliography for the sources used throughout the project.
