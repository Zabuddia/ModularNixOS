Project Purpose

I created this website as a project to learn more about minimalist art and to practice building a site in a minimalist style. The pages summarize key ideas from articles and museum resources, and they connect those ideas to simple digital artwork.

Design Choices

The design of this site follows minimalist principles:

  • Limited color palette: mostly black, white, and gray.
  • Simple typography: one clean sans-serif font, no decorative type.
  • Generous empty space: wide margins and short lines of text.
  • Clear structure: a small number of pages with straightforward navigation.
  • No visual clutter: no unnecessary effects or animations.

These choices are inspired by writing on minimalism in art and design, which emphasizes clarity, reduction, and balance.

How to Read This Site

You can move through the pages in any order, but one simple path is:

  • Start at What Is Minimalism? for the basic context.
  • Visit Key Artists to see an example of a minimalist artist.
  • Look at the Gallery to see minimalist-inspired studies.
  • Read Style & Meaning to think about how minimalist ideas work.
  • Check the Bibliography to see the sources behind the summaries.
